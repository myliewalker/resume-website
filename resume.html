Work Experience

Gruss Lipper Biophotonics Lab

Research Intern

June 2017 - August 2017

I developed and tested a near-infrared biosensor. This biosensor detects the activity of RhoA, a protein whose functionality is important for many different types of cancer. Unlike previous biosensors, it uses near-infrared proteins and therefore allows for the visualization of tumors and cells in living organisms. After I designed a preliminary biosensor, I tested its efficiency by measuring the proteins' light emissions. These results showed that although it transferred light efficiently, the proteins were not as abundant as they should have been. In order to improve the biosensor's functionality, my mentor and I decided to change its structure to mimic that of a different near-infrared biosensor. This second version was significantly better - the protein levels were higher, and the efficiency was nearly comparable to both the original version, and current RhoA biosensors. Based on these results, my mentor is working to combine the aspects of both these devices and develop an optimized version.

After reading this sequence, I identified the genotype of each individual and determined whether each individual was genetically susceptible to TB. Innocence Project

Summer Intern

June 2015 - July 2015

The Innocence Project is a nonprofit organization that works to exonerate the wrongfully convicted. I helped organize information regarding eyewitness identification and defendants' confessions. Both of these pieces of evidence are very compelling to juries, and are typically integral parts of proving a defendant guilty. However, the organization is working to examine cases in which these fail, because either witnesses misidentify the guilty party or defendants falsely confess. I helped organize the office's library system and added additional articles that discuss the issues of eyewitness misidentification give false confessions. I also identified eyewitness identification and false testimony experts that the lawyers could cross-examine in upcoming trials. During my internship, I also got to watch part of a trial and heard from a guest speaker who discussed his organization's goal of halving the population in correctional facilities by 2030. This internship helped give me insight into the value of consulting with individuals in other fields and the importance of strong client connections in a workplace.

Uruguayan Consulate

Summer Intern

July 2015 - August 2015

The Uruguayan Consulate aims to help and connect the New York Uruguayan community. Their services are twofold: they help individuals who recently emigrated to the United States obtain higher education and finding work opportunities, and also connect them with New York residents through cultural events and programs. During the weeks that I worked there, I helped with the consulate’s outreach and finances. I researched information about institutions with Latin American Studies Department and Uruguayan events in New York. By doing this, I aimed to identify universities to that these might be interested in studying at and help the consulate plan future events by identifying potential collaboration partners. I also reviewed the consulate’s expenses to ensure that costs of travel and planning were documented accurately and thoroughly. Through this work I was able to learn about the multitude of different services that can facilitate these transitions and became interested in learning more how different fields such as the financial and tech industries can support them.
